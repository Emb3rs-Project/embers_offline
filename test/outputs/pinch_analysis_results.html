What is a Pinch Analysis?

In brief, the pinch analysis is a theoretical method that, based on fundamental thermodynamics, analyzes the heat flow through the industry’s streams with the aim of recovering heat within those streams by proposing a heat exchanger - HX - network. By reducing the heating/cooling needs, it also reduces fuel costs and CO2 emissions (for more information check EMB3Rs deliverable D.3.3).

What can you obtain from this report?

The simulation assesses heat recovery alternatives within the streams by performing a series of pinch analysis to the streams combinations. The output of this routine is the 3 best pinch designs in terms of CO2 emission reductions/savings (kgCO2/kWh), amount fo heat recovered (kW), and heat recovery specific cost (€/kW).

What is the report structure?

It is presented:

  • A table with the streams and the minimum heat exchangers ΔT, the user provided. Below the streams table, the user can check whether all possbile combinations of streams were analyzed or not. If not the list of infeasible streams combinations is shown.
  • An overview of the designed solutions for each of the categories - CO2 emissions savings, heat recovery, and heat recovery specific cost). The 3 best solutions of each category are shown to the user.
  • A detailed analysis of each designed solution of each category.

2. Overview Designed Solutions

Here you can find an overview on the best 3 designed solutions for each one of the analyzed categories

Categories analyzed:

  • CO2 Savings - Yearly CO2 emissions reduction
  • Heat Recovery - Yearly heat recovered
  • Heat Recovery Specific Cost - Solution's CAPEX over yearly heat recovered

For the three categories analyzed there can be a repetition on the designed solutions - check the ID. There will always be heat recovered in all solutions since the pinch analysis aims to recover heat, however, there may not be Monetary/CO2 Savings if streams without an equipment and fuel associated are provided.

In each category, you can find for each solution:

  • CO2 Savings - yearly CO2 emissions reduction
  • Monetary Savings - Yearly monetary savings
  • Heat Recovered - Yearly heat recovered
  • CAPEX - Solution investment cost
  • OM Fix - Fixed operation and maintenance costs of the solution

HXs Network Diagram

In this subsection it is provided an economic analysis on the designed heat exchangers

  • The following diagram proposes a heat exchanger (HX) network.
  • The streams are represented by the horizontal lines with the respective IDs next to them.
  • Red lines are hot streams, which are streams going from a larger to a smaller temperature. Blue lines are cold streams, which are streams going from a smaller to a larger temperature.
  • Stream splits (splitting the stream flowrate) may occur to guarantee the achievement of a final solution. The splits are represented as the diagonal lines going out of the main/original stream.
  • The heat exchangers designed are represented with its ID, together with the estimated power. In some cases a temperature may appear next to the HX, representing the temperature the stream reached. If not, it means it reached the target or the pinch temperature.
  • Cold/Hot Utility - An external source of heating/cooling additional to the heat recovered between streams, so that they reach their target temperature.
